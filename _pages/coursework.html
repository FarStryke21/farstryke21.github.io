Spring 2024

Trustworthy AI Autonomy
This advanced course addresses the critical challenge of ensuring safety in AI-driven autonomy, covering topics like adversarial defense, generative models, and safe reinforcement learning. It equips students with research skills to explore cutting-edge trustworthy AI methods and their applications in real-world systems like self-driving cars and healthcare devices. Designed for research-oriented students in machine learning, robotics, and human-machine interaction, the course fosters team-based research capabilities essential for developing secure and reliable autonomous systems.
Instructor: Dr. Ding Zhao
Learning for 3D Vision
This course focuses on integrating 3D perception and learning techniques crucial for autonomous agents operating in complex environments. It explores advancements like differentiable rendering and single-view 3D prediction, essential for applications spanning robotics, self-driving, virtual reality, and computer vision.
Instructor: Dr. Shumbham Tulsiani

Fall 2023

Planning and Decision making in Robotics
This course delves into algorithmic techniques essential for planning and decision-making in robotics, encompassing path planning, motion planning, and task planning across various robotic platforms. It explores real-world applications in ground and aerial robots, humanoids, mobile manipulation, and multi-robot systems, enhancing understanding and implementation of autonomous robotic capabilities.
Instructor: Dr. Maxim Likhachev
Introduction to Robot Learning
This course explores essential topics in robot learning, focusing on machine/deep/reinforcement learning and visual learning for decision-making in diverse environments. Students will gain proficiency in reinforcement learning techniques (model-based, model-free), imitation learning, visual cognition, and simulation-based policy transfer, with insights from neuroscience and psychology informing cognitive approaches in decision making.
Instructor: Dr. Deepak Pathak

Spring 2023

Mechatronics Design
This course focuses on integrating mechanical, electronic, and computer control systems through hands-on laboratory projects. It covers operational principles and system design for mechatronic systems, emphasizing practical implementation and theoretical understanding.
Instructor: Dr. Cameron Riviere and Dr. Zeynep Temel
Optimal Control and Reinforcement Learning
This course explores methods for enhancing robotic motion and interaction in diverse environments, covering nonlinear dynamics, optimal control, state estimation, and reinforcement learning. Students gain practical skills through hands-on applications across different robotic systems, enabling them to apply these concepts effectively in research and development.
Instructor: Dr. Zachary Manchester

Fall 2022

Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence
This course covers foundational machine learning and AI techniques essential for engineers, including Bayesian learning, neural networks, SVMs, clustering, regression, and optimization. Emphasis is on theoretical foundations and mathematical modeling to equip students with robust problem-solving skills for data-intensive applications.
Instructor: Dr. Amir Barati Farimani
Modern Control Theory
This course provides a hands-on approach to model-based control design for linear systems, covering state-variable modeling, linear algebra fundamentals, and design techniques like LQR, MPC, and Kalman filters. Students gain practical skills in analyzing and implementing controllers and observers for dynamic systems.
Instructor: Prof. Mark Bedellion
Computer Vision
This course introduces foundational concepts and practical applications of computer vision, covering sensor selection, image processing, motion analysis, 3D reconstruction, point cloud processing, feature tracking, and object detection. Students gain theoretical and practical knowledge essential for applying computer vision in diverse engineering fields like automation, robotics, and medical imaging.
Instructor: Dr. Kenji Shimada

Audits and Extra Courses

Localization and Mapping
This course delves into the challenges of robot localization, mapping, and SLAM, focusing on efficient probabilistic inference methods and state-of-the-art systems. Students gain insights into handling sensor uncertainty and achieving real-time localization and mapping in mobile robotics applications.
Instructor: Dr. Michael Kaess
Prototyping for Equity
This course integrates prototyping techniques with safe operation of fabrication tools like 3D printers, laser cutters, and power tools. Students apply the engineering design cycle to create products tailored for underserved user populations, emphasizing practical skills in fabrication and design iteration.
Instructor: Dr. Diana Haidar
Manual Machining
This course familiarizes students with the operation and safety of machine tools. This gives students knowledge of what goes into engineering designs in building a prototype and also enables them to operate shop machinery as a part of future courses.
